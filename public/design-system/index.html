The Problem

In early 2022, my Technical Lead and I were tasked with finding a way to drive more users to the Observability platform during onboarding. I lead the project alongside an Engineering Technical Lead. We worked across the Observability organization as well as with the Elastic UI design system team to establish cohesion throughout the product. The following case study shows the end to end product design effort which resulted in a Saved Views feature being implemented in the Observability Logs solution.

Impact

  • Frustrating Developer experience due to the lack of a Design System or guidelines.
  • Bottlenecks between design + engineering make it hard to move quickly and iterate.
  • Inconsistent UI doesn't inspire a sense of safety and trust for users.
  • Competitors products are more polished and they are beating us even though our feature offering is more complete.

Auditing the current design

  • Inconsistent use of color, components, and patterns.
  • No clear guidelines or patterns for engineers when building.
  • Product doesn't feel like a developer tool.
  • The design / look doesn't match the quality of the features offered.

Release Plan

In order to iterate quickly and bring more value to our users sooner, we opted to release the updates in a phased rollout using feature flag driven development.

General Audience Release

For GA, we updated chunks of the product at the time based on components and used feature flags to test with certain segments of users. Six weeks before GA, we flipped on the new styles for our internal organization. This allowed us to continue to test and gather feedback leading up to release.

Our GTM team helped us recruit customers who were willing to test out the new UI. This allowed us to release small chunks at a time and get feedback in real time. We conducted live and async sessions and gathered feedback in a shared Slack channel.
