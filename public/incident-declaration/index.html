Incident Declaration

Re-imagining the Incident Declaration experience in the web UI to provide a streamlined and improved experience.

The Product

FireHydrant

My Role

Lead Product Designer

The Team

Head of Product, Technical Lead

Timeframe

Dec 2022 - Feb 2023

The Problems

User Experience

One of the main value points of the product FireHydrant product is the ability to declare an incident in seconds. At this time a majority of our users relied on our Slack app, but the ones that used the FireHydrant web UI had a feature limited experience.

Business

Large orgs were hesitant to adopt widely adopt FireHydrant because of the gap in user experience between Slack and the web UI.Orgs that didn't use Slack were not confident in choosing FireHydrant as their Incident Management solution because of the shortcomings of the web UI particularly in regards to Incident Declaration.

Audit of current experience

  • Unclear path for users.
  • Required v. not required sections.
  • Help text is inconsistent and confusing.
  • Form is long and not conducive for taking quick action.

Challenges

  • Improve experience without removing features or complexity
  • Feature parity with current Slack experience
  • Ability to choose an incident type
  • Optimize user flow, IA, & layout
  • Create a resolved incident
  • Create reusable patterns for all forms that will be used across the product
