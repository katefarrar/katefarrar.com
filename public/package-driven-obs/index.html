Summary

In early 2022, my Technical Lead and I were tasked with finding a way to drive more users to the Observability platform during onboarding. I lead the project alongside an Engineering Technical Lead. We worked across the Observability organization as well as with the Elastic UI design system team to establish cohesion throughout the product. The following case study shows the end to end product design effort which resulted in a Saved Views feature being implemented in the Observability Logs solution.

Research

I conducted five, hour-long user interviews with a mix a of internal users, external users, and Solutions Architecrs. I used Zoom to conduct the interviews and the Dovetail platform to synthesize interviews and pull out themes.

Challenges

Guiding the user without asking leading questions that will simply validate our hypothesis. Getting feedback on the current experience while still allowing for the user to present their ideal experience.

Key Findings

  • Users want to see their data in context out of the box without having to build complex Dashboards.
  • Current experience is disjointed. Onboarding doesn't flow well into Observability solution.
  • Different roles have different needs.
  • Users range from beginners to "power users"

Design Workshop

After synthesizing the findings from research, I lead a Design Workshop with my Technical Lead and a Senior Engineer on our team. Using a combination of Design Thinking and Human Cenetered Design methodologies, I focused our time around four areas:

  1. Empathizing with our users
  2. Defining the problem
  3. Ideating on solutions
  4. Understanding technical limitations

How might we provide a way for users to see the data they are in ingesting in context without having to set up Dashboards or complex filters?

The outcome of the workshop was a well defined problem statement, several concepts to test, and a better understanding of technical limitations and concepts.

Early Concepts

The next step after the workshop was to spend some time designing to test the concepts that we had explored. Because we wanted to move quickly and were still in early exploration, I opted to do lo-fidelity mockups with limited detail that focused on the user interactions.
